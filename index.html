Botanical Name :Calotropis gigantea / Calotropis procera
Family Name: Apocynaceae
Local Name: Arka
Vernacular Name: Hindi – Aak / Madar , English – Giant milkweed / Crown flower
Synonyms : Arka, Alarka, Mandara, Vasuka
Classical categorization:
➢ Described in Ayurveda as a Upavisha (semi-poisonous plant)
➢ Included under Kshara dravya and Tikshna dravyas
Habitat:: Widely found throughout India in dry, wastelands, roadsides, and sandy soils; thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.
Morphology :
➢ EA large, erect shrub (2–4 meters tall)
➢ Leaves: Opposite, sessile, thick, oval, and covered with waxy coating
➢ Stem: Ash-colored, branched, contains milky latex
➢ Flowers: White or lavender, waxy appearance
➢ Fruits: Follicles, inflated and green
➢ Latex: Milky, sticky, and toxic
Useful part:
➢ Root bark
➢ Leaves
➢ Flowers
➢ Latex
Chemical constituents :
➢ Cardiac glycosides (Calotropin, Uscharin, Calotoxin)
➢ Flavonoids
➢ Triterpenoids
➢ Proteolytic enzymes
➢ Resins
Actions :
➢ Analgesic
➢ Anti-inflammatory
➢ Antimicrobial
➢ Digestive stimulant
➢ Purgative
➢ Expectorant
Properties:
➢ Skin diseases (e.g., eczema, ringworm)
➢ Respiratory disorders (asthma, cough)
➢ Digestive issues (constipation, indigestion)
➢ Joint pain and swelling
➢ Worm infestations
Indications :
➢ Skin diseases (e.g., eczema, ringworm)
➢ Respiratory disorders (asthma, cough)
➢ Digestive issues (constipation, indigestion)
➢ Joint pain and swelling
➢ Worm infestations
Therapeutic administration:
➢ External: Latex applied on skin diseases, wounds (with caution)
➢ Internal: Processed root bark or leaf preparations used under supervision
➢ Leaves warmed and applied for pain relief
Dose :
➢ Root bark powder: 125–250 mg
➢ Latex: Very small quantities (only under expert supervision due to toxicity)
Formulations :
➢ Arka Kshara
➢ Arka Taila
➢ Arka Lavana
➢ Arka Ghrita
